## Part 1: The wordplay philosophy

### 1.1 The truth-only rule

Every word on this page must be one of the following:

1. **A verifiable fact.** "$497 flat fee." "Amazon suspended over 266,000 seller accounts in Q1 2025." "Competitors charge $1,500 to $3,000." These are true, sourced from real data, and can withstand a skeptical seller Googling them.

2. **A structural description of what we do.** "We classify your violation type." "We draft your Plan of Action in Amazon's required 3-part format." These describe the actual process and are true by definition.

3. **A forward-looking commitment backed by money.** "Up to 3 revision rounds included." "50% refund if no progress after 2 full attempts." These are promises, not past performance claims. The seller can hold us to them because we're putting money on the line.

Nothing on this page falls outside these three categories. No fabricated statistics. No inflated case counts. No unverifiable success rates. No "12,400 sellers helped." No "95% reinstatement rate." No "$45M revenue recovered."

### 1.2 Why this matters for this specific audience

Amazon sellers are the most skeptical buyer demographic you can target. They have been:

- Burned by Amazon's opaque automated enforcement system
- Burned by competitors who charged $1,500 to $3,000 and delivered template letters
- Active in Facebook groups where they warn each other about fraudulent services
- Conditioned to distrust any claim that cannot be independently verified

One person screenshotting a fake "92% success rate" and posting "has anyone actually used Appeal Edge?" in an Amazon seller Facebook group will destroy credibility if nobody can confirm it. The internet has a sixth sense for fakes, and this community is sharper than most.

### 1.3 What replaces social proof at stage zero

Since Appeal Edge is launching with zero or near-zero completed cases, the page uses four alternative trust mechanisms:

**Process transparency.** When you can't show outcomes, show the machine. The 4-step pipeline described in Amazon-specific language (violation types, ASINs, Root Cause → Corrective Actions → Preventive Measures) proves domain expertise through specificity. No competitor explains their process this clearly because they don't have one worth showing.

**Live demonstration of competence.** The free scan lets the seller paste their suspension notice and watch the AI correctly classify it in 30 seconds. This live proof of capability is more powerful than 100 testimonials.

**Guarantees that put our money on the line.** "3 revision rounds included" and "50% refund if no progress after 2 full attempts" are more credible than any claimed success rate because they transfer risk from the buyer to us.

**Verifiable industry facts.** "266,000 sellers suspended in Q1 2025." "Competitors charge $1,500 to $3,000." These are independently confirmable numbers that frame our value proposition without requiring any data about our own performance.

### 1.4 The copy voice

Short sentences. 12 to 18 words average. The reader is stressed and skimming.

Lead with outcomes, not process. "Get reinstated in 48 hours" before "Here is how our AI works."

Use numbers. "$497" is more credible than "affordable." "48 hours" is more credible than "fast." "3 revisions" is more credible than "we'll keep working until it's right."

No exclamation marks. Confidence is quiet. Desperation uses exclamation marks.

No em dashes or hyphens as punctuation connectors. Use commas, periods, or restructure.

No superlatives. Never say "best," "industry-leading," "world-class," or "unmatched." Let the price comparison do the talking.

First person in CTAs. "Get MY free case analysis" not "Get a free case analysis." First person creates psychological ownership.

Mirror the seller's language. Amazon sellers say "suspension notice," "Plan of Action," "Seller Performance team," "ASINs." Use their vocabulary, not marketing vocabulary.

## Part 3: Free scan widget — output specification

### 3.1 Strategy

The free scan is the single most important conversion tool on the page. It serves three purposes simultaneously:

1. **Proof of competence.** The seller watches the AI correctly classify their violation and list the exact evidence they need. This replaces traditional social proof.

2. **Reciprocity.** We give substantial value (a personalized case analysis) before asking for money. This creates a psychological obligation.

3. **Conversion bridge.** The scan makes the seller feel informed about their problem but not equipped to solve it alone. The gap between "understanding what you need" and "writing a POA that presents it correctly" is what drives the purchase.

### 3.2 What the scan gives away (everything)

The scan is completely ungated. No email required. No account creation. No hidden content. The seller gets:

- Full violation type classification
- Case complexity assessment
- Detailed case synthesis paragraph
- Complete evidence requirements list
- Competitor price comparison
- Specific "what happens next" preview

### 3.3 What the scan does NOT include

- **No fabricated win rate percentages.** No "70% industry average / 92% with Appeal Edge" rings. These are unverifiable and destroy credibility.
- **No fabricated claims about Appeal Edge's track record.** No "Appeal Edge wins 92% of cases like this."
- **No urgency text that manufactures false scarcity.** No countdown timers, no "only 3 slots left."

### 3.5 AI prompt guidelines

The system prompt for the scan analysis must include these constraints:

```
YOU ARE an Amazon suspension case analyst. Your job is to read a seller's
suspension notice and produce a structured case analysis.

YOU MUST:
- Identify the specific violation type from the notice
- Extract and reference specific ASINs, complainant names, and complaint IDs
- List the exact evidence documents needed for a successful appeal
- Assess case complexity as Straightforward, Moderate, or Complex
- Write a 2-3 sentence synthesis of the case in plain language
- Generate a factual urgency statement about consequences of inaction
- Preview what a Plan of Action would cover for this case type

YOU MUST NOT:
- Claim any win rate or success percentage for Appeal Edge
- Reference Appeal Edge's track record or number of cases handled
- Use phrases like "we win X% of cases like this"
- Fabricate statistics of any kind
- Create false urgency or scarcity ("limited slots", countdowns)
- Make promises about specific outcomes ("guaranteed reinstatement")
- Use marketing language or superlatives

YOUR TONE: Direct, knowledgeable, specific. You are demonstrating deep
understanding of Amazon's enforcement system, not selling a service.
The analysis itself is the selling.
```
