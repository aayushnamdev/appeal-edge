    We handle all 8 major suspension types.

    Select your suspension type to see how we approach it.

    Inauthentic / Counterfeit

    Most common · ~35% of suspensions

    Triggered when Amazon flags your product sourcing documentation as insufficient. Often happens to resellers, wholesale sellers, or anyone without a direct brand authorization letter. It does not mean Amazon thinks you're selling fakes.

    What Amazon looks for

    Invoices from authorized distributors showing your business name, product UPCs, and purchase dates within the last 365 days. A root cause that acknowledges the documentation gap. Preventive measures showing new supplier vetting procedures.

    Our approach

    We analyze which ASINs were flagged, identify the specific documentation gaps, and draft a POA that addresses each one with the language Amazon's Seller Performance team responds to.

    Get a free analysis for this case type →

    Performance Metrics

    ~20% of suspensions

    Amazon suspends accounts when Order Defect Rate exceeds 1%, Late Shipment Rate exceeds 4%, or Pre-fulfillment Cancel Rate exceeds 2.5%. These thresholds are strict and often triggered by supply chain disruptions or seasonal volume spikes.

    What Amazon looks for

    A clear root cause for each metric that exceeded the threshold. Evidence of corrective actions already taken, not planned. Specific operational changes with dates.

    Our approach

    We pull the specific metrics from your notice, map each one to its root cause, and build a POA with operational specifics: dates of changes, systems implemented, and measurable targets.

    Get a free analysis for this case type →

    IP / Trademark / Copyright

    ~15% of suspensions

    A brand owner filed an intellectual property complaint against your listing. Some are legitimate, many are weaponized by competitors. Amazon removes first and asks questions later.

    What Amazon looks for

    Evidence that you are authorized to sell the product, or that the IP claim is invalid. A retraction from the rights owner is the fastest path. Otherwise, a detailed counter-notice with documentation.

    Our approach

    We assess whether the complaint is valid, draft a counter-notice if warranted, and where possible, help facilitate a retraction from the rights owner directly.

    Get a free analysis for this case type →

    Related Account

    ~10% of suspensions

    Amazon detected a link between your account and another account that was previously suspended. Links can be shared IP addresses, payment methods, devices, WiFi networks, or shipping addresses.

    What Amazon looks for

    A clear explanation of the relationship or lack thereof. Evidence that the accounts are operated by separate, independent businesses. Documentation of distinct ownership and separate operational infrastructure.

    Our approach

    Related account cases require extremely precise documentation. We identify the likely linkage point and build a POA that addresses it with supporting evidence of independent operations.

    Get a free analysis for this case type →

    Review Manipulation

    ~8% of suspensions

    Amazon's algorithms flagged your account for suspected review solicitation, incentivized reviews, or unusual review velocity. This often catches sellers running legitimate product launches where review patterns trigger automated detection.

    What Amazon looks for

    Acknowledgment of any activity that could have appeared as manipulation. Evidence of policy compliance. A clear plan to prevent triggering detection systems.

    Our approach

    We analyze the review patterns Amazon likely flagged, identify what triggered the detection, and build a POA that demonstrates deep understanding of Amazon's review policies.

    Get a free analysis for this case type →

    INFORM Consumers Act

    ~5% of suspensions

    US sellers who haven't submitted required identity verification under the INFORM Consumers Act. Amazon is legally required to collect and verify this information for high-volume sellers.

    What Amazon looks for

    Completed identity verification: government-issued ID, bank statement, and tax documentation matching your seller account.

    Our approach

    INFORM cases are documentation-driven. We ensure your verification package is complete, formatted correctly, and addresses any discrepancies Amazon flagged.

    Get a free analysis for this case type →

    Fair Pricing

    ~4% of suspensions

    Amazon's pricing algorithm flagged your prices as significantly higher than external benchmarks. Common during peak seasons when fulfillment fee increases tempt sellers to raise prices beyond competitive bands.

    What Amazon looks for

    Cost documentation justifying the price. Supplier invoices, fulfillment fee breakdowns, competitive pricing alignment. A plan to maintain fair pricing going forward.

    Our approach

    We build a cost-traceable pricing justification showing exactly why your price is where it is, mapped against Amazon's fee structure and comparable market prices.

    Get a free analysis for this case type →

    Restricted Products

    ~3% of suspensions

    Selling in gated categories or listing products that require pre-approval without authorization. Includes hazmat, dietary supplements, certain electronics, and seasonal restricted categories.

    What Amazon looks for

    Product compliance documentation: safety certifications, lab tests, manufacturer declarations. Evidence of proper category ungating.

    Our approach

    We identify which products triggered the restriction, gather the required compliance documentation, and draft a POA that addresses Amazon's product safety requirements.
